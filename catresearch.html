Some notes on my research into current robot and AI pets

Angle, Colin & Familiar Machines

Colin Angle debuted his new company Familiar Machines & Magic at the WSJ Future of Everything on 4 May 2026. Familiars is a bear cub-shaped four legged robot with touch sensitive fur and audio "ears", designed not to talk, but to be hugged, walked, and lived with. As my final project related to themes of pets and companionship, this prototype gives me a contemporary case study of what happens when emotional design meets robotics, and who is the AI pet really for.

AIBO-Sony's robotic dog

Sony's AIBO debuted in 1999 as the first consumer robot pet: a small robotic dog with sensors, motor joints, and a programmed personality. The 2018 reboot added deep learning, cloud sync, and a companion phone app, so AIBO could "remember" its owner and develop habits over time. AIBO is the direct ancestor of every AI pet that came after, including Familiar Machines' Daphne. As I work on a code driven cat, looking at AIBO's three decades of historic evolution helps me see the long arc of trying to make machines feel alive.
